Work Completed Along Cedar Lane In Highland Park On Water Pipeline Construction Project

Middlesex Water Company has commenced construction of a new raw water pipeline from its Pump Station in New Brunswick, near the Raritan River to its Water Treatment Plant in Edison. The pipeline is being constructed to ensure backup water supply and additional high quality dinking water for customers in northern Middlesex County. The construction of the 60” diameter pipeline, which is being performed by the water company’s contractor Northeast Remsco Construction, Inc., started in June 2004 and will be complete in April 2005. 

 

As of August 16, 2004, installation on Cedar Lane from Johnson Park in Highland Park at the intersection of River Road to the north towards the Edison Township line has been completed.  The project within Cedar Lane included pavement restoration, pavement restriping, and restoration of the original traffic signalization pattern.  As planned, the construction within Cedar Lane was completed within the summer months to avoid school-related traffic during the fall school season.

From mid-September 2004 to early October 2004, piping will be installed in off-road areas near the intersection of Cedar Lane and Road 1 towards the water company’s Water Treatment Plant.  Also, at this time and until early November 2004, the pipeline will be installed within Johnson Park and underneath the Raritan River and Route 18 to the water company’s Pump Station.  During this work, the motoring public should not be affected significantly by this construction activity.
