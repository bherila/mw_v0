Delaware PSC Issues Final Decision On Tidewater Rate Case - Current Rates in Effect Will Not Change

CONTACT: Gerard L. Esposito, President, Tidewater Utilities, Inc. (302) 734-7500 or Bernadette Sohler, Director of Communications (732) 634-1500

DOVER, DE (October 28, 2004) -- Tidewater Utilities Inc., (Tidewater), a water utility serving 25,000 residential and other customers in New Castle, Kent and Sussex Counties, DE, has received the final decision on the rate case it had filed with the Delaware Public Service Commission (PSC) in April 2004. The Company, as allowed under the PSC rules and regulations, had placed interim rates of 15% into effect in June 2004 to cover its additional increases and capital costs. In its decision and Order, the PSC granted the Company an increase equal to these interim rates. No additional increases have been granted. The 15% increase reflects rates currently in effect.

Tidewater had sought a 24% increase or $2,527,887 over present revenues. The interim increase of 15% represents $1,500,000 above annual revenues inclusive of two Distribution System Improvement Charge (DSIC) adjustments already implemented by Tidewater over the past year. A DSIC is a charge for non-revenue producing capital improvements. Tidewater has invested more than $48 million to build new water systems, upgrade treatment facilities and improve its water distribution infrastructure, since 1992.

Tidewater will be eligible for a second phase increase, and able to apply for additional revenues of up to $533,598, provided it completes a number of capital projects by May 2005. The Company also agreed to waive its right to file a DSIC application over the next three six-month cycles (January and July of 2005, and January of 2006). Tidewater also agreed to defer making an application for a general rate increase until after April 1, 2006.
Tidewater has been systematically investing in plant upgrades and equipment over the past several years to continue providing quality service and to keep pace with double-digit growth in its customer base. The Company continues to focus on creating system interconnections, water main extensions and consolidating its many independent systems into regional districts. Tidewater operates 70 independent water systems that serve over 240 residential communities throughout Delaware.

Presently, the average household served by Tidewater is charged approximately $34.20 per month for 5,000 gallons of water. Water charges for residential customers consist of a $43.37 quarterly facilities charge and a consumption charge of $ 3.9493 per 1,000 gallons.

“We are committed to investing in our water infrastructure to ensure a safe and reliable drinking water supply for all Delawareans. Although this increase is less than we requested, the added revenues will help support the upgrades needed to continue providing quality service to our customers,” said Gerard L. Esposito, President of Tidewater Utilities, Inc. “The final decision balances the interests of the Company and its ratepayers. It avoids the cost of further litigation and delays the time when the Company can file again for a rate increase,” added Esposito.

Tidewater Utilities, Inc. is a wholly owned subsidiary of Middlesex Water Company, (NASDAQ:MSEX) a leading New Jersey water utility established in 1897, serving residents of central New Jersey.

Middlesex Water Company Reports Third Quarter Earnings

CONTACT: Bernadette Sohler, Director of Communications (732) 634-1500

ISELIN, NJ (October 28, 2004) -- Middlesex Water Company, (NASDAQ:MSEX), a provider of water and wastewater services in New Jersey and Delaware, announced today that its net income, for the quarter ended September 30, 2004, increased by 40.5% over the prior year.

Third Quarter 2004 Results
Consolidated operating revenues, for the quarter ended September 30, 2004, were $19.9 million, up $2.3 million, from $17.6 million for the same period in 2003. Water sales improved by $1.1 million in our New Jersey systems, which was primarily a result of base rate increases. Continued double-digit customer growth and base rate increases for the Company’s Delaware subsidiary, Tidewater Utilities, Inc. (TUI) generated an additional $0.8 million in revenues, and our meter installation venture contributed $0.6 million to revenues. Revenues from our City of Perth Amboy contract decreased by $0.3 million. Operating expenses increased to $15.4 million, up from $14.1 million. Costs associated with the meter installation venture were $0.5 million of the increase. Higher costs for purchased water, energy, payroll and benefits, insurance and corporate governance fees for the Middlesex system represented $0.2 million of the increase. Income and other taxes increased a combined $0.7 million.
The Company reported third quarter 2004 earnings applicable to common stock increased to $3.3 million, compared to $2.3 million in the prior year, and earnings per share of common stock increased from $0.22 to $0.29.

Nine-Month Results
For the nine months ended September 30, 2004, revenues were $53.5 million, up $4.9 million, from $48.6 million for the same period in the prior year. Water sales improved by $1.7 million in our New Jersey systems, which was primarily a result of base rate increases. Customer growth of 10.7% and base rate increases for TUI generated an additional $1.6 million in revenues, while our meter installation venture added $1.8 million to revenues. Revenues from our City of Perth Amboy contract decreased by $0.4 million due to scheduled reductions in fixed fees. Operating expenses increased to $43.7 million, up from $39.6 million. Twelve-Month Results
For the twelve months ended September 30, 2004, revenues were $69.0 million, up from $63.8 million for the same period in the prior year. The double-digit customer growth and base rate increases for TUI generated an additional $2.1 million in revenues, while our meter installation venture added $2.0 million to revenues. Water sales by the Middlesex system increased by $1.2 million. This was the result of $1.5 million of additional revenues from base rate increases which were partially offset by reduced consumption revenues of $0.3 million due to wet weather. Revenues from our City of Perth Amboy contract decreased by $0.4 million. Operating expenses increased to $56.7 million, up from $51.6 million. Costs associated with the customer growth in Delaware and the meter installation venture were $2.8 million of the increase. Higher costs for purchased water, energy, water treatment, payroll and benefits, insurance and corporate governance fees for the Middlesex system represented $1.5 million of the increase. Depreciation expense increased $0.5 million as a result of additional utility plant in service. As a result, earnings applicable to common stock were $7.2 million, which was less than 1% higher than the same period in 2003, however earnings per share of common stock decreased from $0.69 or $0.67 due to the higher number of shares outstanding that resulted from the issuance of 700,000 shares in May 2004.

Middlesex Water Company Coordinates Colonia Water Main Cleaning and Lining with Township Road Work

CONTACT: Bernadette Sohler, Director of Communications (732) 634-1500

ISELIN, NJ (August 30, 2004) -- In advance of road reconstruction and sanitary sewer replacement work being conducted by Woodbridge Township, Middlesex Water Company will be cleaning and lining an additional 2,000 feet of cast iron water pipe on Predmore Avenue in the Colonia section of Woodbridge, beginning in September.

This past April, the Company began its 2004 RENEW Program, a rehabilitative effort to clean and line 8 miles of unlined water mains, in the Fords section of Woodbridge, the Bonhamtown section of Edison, and other areas within its service area. After discussing the Township’s road reconstruction schedule with officials, and in the interest of minimizing road openings and ongoing inconvenience to area residents, Middlesex Water added Predmore Avenue, a targeted area for future RENEW work, to its 2004 Program. The water main work will be completed prior to road restoration efforts.

The RENEW Program involves cleaning the accumulated scale from older sections of cast iron pipe and installing a new lining.   The project essentially helps to extend the life of older pipe and helps to improve overall water quality and service while strengthening the water distribution infrastructure.  Work will be performed on Predmore Avenue in Colonia, from Sterling Drive to Neptune Place, and is expected to be completed by October 1, 2004.

To provide continual water service, Middlesex Water will erect a temporary water system including hydrants and water service connections aboveground and maintain this system while the mains are being cleaned and lined.  Aboveground piping will be visible along the curbs on both sides of the street.  The RENEW Program should not interfere with the delivery of water.  Customers can expect to receive continuous water service, except for the few minutes when service is being transferred to and from temporary lines, or when maintenance on the temporary line is required.

Anyone with questions about the RENEW Program or those experiencing extended interruption of service as a result of work being done should call Middlesex Water Company at 732-634-1500 and ask to speak with a RENEW program representative.

Middlesex Water Company Reminds Customers About Safety

CONTACT: Bernadette Sohler, Director of Communications (732) 634-1500

ISELIN, NJ (August 18, 2004) -- Middlesex Water Company is urging customers to be cautious about opening their doors to imposters claiming to work for the water company. 

“We’ve recently heard of imposters who are posing as water personnel as a ruse to gain entrance into a resident’s home and commit robbery, said Ronald F. Williams, P.E., Chief Operating Officer of Middlesex Water.  “They may say they need to gain entrance to the home to conduct water testing or perform meter operations.  The Company is urging all customers to be smart and safe by checking for proper photo identification and company uniform before opening their door to a stranger,” said Williams.

According to Williams, most water meter reading is now conducted from outside the home which significantly reduces the need for water personnel to enter a customer’s home.   “When inside meter access is necessary, such as during meter inspections or changeouts, appointments are typically pre-arranged with the homeowner, so they know when to expect a service representative,” said Williams. 

Williams stressed that, at other times, customer visits may be related to unexpected outages and are therefore, not planned in advance.  “In those cases such as when a boil water recommendation is in place due to a service outage, a company representative may need to visit homes in the vicinity of the outage to obtain samples of the customers’ water,” said Williams. “This sample gathering at the customer’s tap is necessary to ensure that water, restored after the outage, meets all state and federal water quality regulations.”   “To be on the safe side, we instruct our crews to always carry proper identification and we encourage our customers to request their credentials,” said Williams. “It’s an important safety precaution that’s worth the effort.”

Mr. Williams also stressed that Middlesex Water does not collect cash outside the office under any circumstances – nor does it provide refunds on a door-to-door basis, a ruse often used by imposters.  Customers with any doubts about their water service representative’s identity should call Customer Service at (732) 634-1500.

Middlesex Water Company to Invest $3.8 Million Rehabilitating Water Mains in Woodbridge and Edison

CONTACT: Bernadette Sohler, Director of Communications
(732) 634-1500

Iselin, NJ (March 26, 2004) – Middlesex Water Company plans to invest $3.8 million in 2004 to enhance water quality and improve water flows in the Fords section of Woodbridge, the Bonhamtown section of Edison, and other areas within its service area this spring, when it begins its RENEW Program, a rehabilitative effort to clean and line unlined water mains.

The RENEW Program involves cleaning the accumulated scale from older sections of cast iron pipe and installing a new lining. The project essentially helps to extend the life of older pipe and helps to improve overall water quality and service while strengthening the water distribution infrastructure. Approximately 7 miles of water main will be rehabilitated beginning in early April. This work will affect individual streets in the project area for a period of 2-3 months.

In Fords, water mains under streets within the area north of King Georges Post Road and Amboy Avenue, south of the New Jersey Turnpike, and west of the Garden State Parkway will be rehabilitated. Work will also be performed on mains on streets on South Main Street between Martin Avenue and Woodbridge Avenue in Bonhamtown.
To provide continual water service, Middlesex Water will erect a temporary water system including hydrants and water service connections aboveground and maintain this system while the mains are being cleaned and lined. Aboveground piping will be visible along the curbs on both sides of the street.

“Each year, through the RENEW Program, we are reinvesting in our water infrastructure to help us to better maintain the integrity of our distribution system and deliver water to our customers more effectively,” said Ronald F. Williams, Chief Operating Officer. “Since we initiated the RENEW Program, 51 miles of water mains have been rehabilitated. Throughout our 730-mile Middlesex system, less than 19% of our water mains remain unlined. We intend to continue this program into the future and are being aided this year by low interest loans obtained through the New Jersey Environmental Infrastructure Trust,” said Mr. Williams.

The RENEW Program should not interfere with the delivery of water. Customers can expect to receive continuous water service, except for the few minutes when service is being transferred to and from temporary lines, or when maintenance on the temporary line is required.

Anyone with questions about the RENEW Program or those experiencing extended interruption of service as a result of work being done should call Middlesex Water Company at 732-634-1500 and ask to speak with a RENEW program representative.

ISELIN, NEW JERSEY (May 7, 2003) -- Middlesex Water Company will be working to enhance water quality and improve water flows in Metuchen and the Bonhamtown section of Edison this spring when it begins its RENEW Program, a rehabilitative effort to clean and line cast iron pipe throughout the project area.

The RENEW Program involves cleaning the accumulated scale from older sections of cast iron pipe and installing a new lining. The project essentially helps to extend the life of older pipe and helps to improve overall water quality and service while strengthening the water distribution infrastructure. Approximately 8.5 miles of water main will be rehabilitated beginning in early May. This work will affect individual streets in the Borough for a period of 2-3 months.

In Metuchen, streets south and west of Lake and Middlesex Avenues will be rehabilitated. Additional streets north of Woodbridge Avenue and south of the Amtrak railroad will be rehabilitated in Metuchen. Additional streets west of Route 1 and between Amboy and Grandview Avenues are scheduled for rehabilitation as well.

To provide continual water service, Middlesex Water will erect a temporary water system, including hydrants and water service connections, above ground and maintain this system while the mains are being cleaned and lined. Above ground piping will be visible along the curbs on both sides of the street.

"The RENEW Program, together with year round maintenance activities like hydrant and distribution system flushing, help us to better maintain the integrity of our distribution system and deliver water to our customers more effectively," said Ronald F. Williams, Vice President of Operations. The project is being funded through low interest loans obtained through the New Jersey Environmental Infrastructure Trust.

The RENEW Program should not interfere with the delivery of water. Customers can expect to receive continuous water service, except for the few minutes when service is being transferred to and from temporary lines, or when maintenance on the temporary line is required.

Middlesex Water Company Announces Organizational Restructuring

CONTACT: Bernadette Sohler, Manager of Communications (732) 634-1500

ISELIN, NJ (January 7, 2003) -- Middlesex Water Company, an Iselin-based investor owned water utility, has announced an organizational restructuring following the retirement of
J. Richard Tompkins, former President and Chief Executive Officer. Mr. Tompkins served as CEO of the Company from 1981 until his retirement in January 2003. He will remain as the Company's Chairman.

Dennis G. Sullivan has been named Chief Executive Officer. Mr. Sullivan was named President of Middlesex Water Company in 2001. He joined the Company in 1984 as Corporate Attorney, was elected Assistant Secretary-Assistant Treasurer in 1988 and Vice President-General Counsel in 1990. He was elected to the Company's Board of Directors in 1999 and was named Senior Vice President and General Counsel in 2000.

Ronald F. Williams has been appointed Chief Operating Officer. Mr. Williams serves as Vice President of Operations and is responsible for operations at Middlesex Water Company and its contract operations subsidiary, Utility Service Affiliates (Perth Amboy) Inc. Mr. Williams joined Middlesex Water in March 1995 as Assistant Vice President and currently oversees the Company's engineering, production, distribution and metering operations.

Richard M. Risoldi has been named Assistant Vice President of Operations to provide added operations support and new business development. Mr. Risoldi, formerly Director of Production, will oversee operations of the Company's subsidiaries including the Pinelands Water and Wastewater Companies, Bayview Water Company, and Utility Service Affiliates, Inc., the Company's non-regulated subsidiary. Mr Risoldi will also be serving as a liaison to Tidewater Utilities, Inc., the Company's subsidiary in Delaware.

"These organizational changes will help us to work effectively in delivering service to our customers and expanding our operations," said Mr. Sullivan. "With the retirement of Mr. Tompkins as CEO, we are building on his legacy of quality service as we face a new chapter in our Company's history. This restructuring will help us to capitalize on the Company's strengths and provide greater integration of all departments within the Middlesex Water family of companies," added Mr. Sullivan.

Middlesex Water Company, founded in 1897, supplies water service for domestic, commercial, industrial and fire protection purposes. For more information, visit www.middlesexwater.com.
